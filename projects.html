Codenames

About

For my honors thesis project I worked closesly with one professor and four other students to recreate the popular board game Codenames. Codenames is a board-game where two teams compete by each having a Spymaster give one-word clues that can point to multiple words on the board. The other players on the team attempt to guess their team's words while avoiding the words of the other team. For our project we decided to test 3 (Word2Vec, Glove and Probabilistic Fast Text) different algorithms and how effective they were in providing accurate clues for a set of words, just like in Codenames. We composed a web application that allowed us to send out our project for testing. Below is a link to our thesis paper which explains our process and results. My role in this project was to help implement and deploy the frontend portion using html/css using HTML and CSS. I also helped help collect our data to formulate our results.

Link to paper


Tech Stack

Python, HTML/CSS


Check out the site!


Results

After our web application was all built, we sent to our fellow peers for testing. The 3 word embeddings/alorigthms all had there strengths and weaknesses. In terms of technical asspects of the project we were able to sussecfully replicate the core mechanics of Codenames.

One Night Werewolf

About

One Night Ultimate Werewolf is a social deduction game in which each player is assigned a specific role and will either be on the werewolf team or the villager team. If you're on the villager team the object of the game is to vote off a werewolf. If you're on the werewolf team the objective is to convince the villagers that you are not a werewolf and to vote off a villager. During my time at CUNY Tech Prep, I worked with a group of 2 other students on a web application based on One Night Ultimate Werewolf. While creating the game we noticed that we needed 5 key features. The web application would have the players sign in, randomly assign roles, have the players complete roles, create a chat room for players to discuss, and finally let the players vote. My role in the project was implementing the fronend side of the project. To do this I used React, HTML/CSS and Socket.io.


Tech Stack

React, HTML, CSS, Socket.io


Check out the github!

Results

Within the two months we worked on this project, my team and I were able to implement all the key features we intended to. I learned a lot about React and how to make dynamic web applications. We then demoed our project to approximately 40-50 people and recieved postive feedback.

Buzz

About

In May of 2018, my team and I developed Buzz at the Spring CUNY Hackathon. Buzz is a AR game made for mobile devices using Unity. The objective of the game is to use real world flowers to collect pollen for your bee hive. The users simply turns AR mode on, points their phones at a flower, and collect the pollen that pops up on the screen. The inspiration behind this was emphasis the importance of bees in our communities, as well as promote fitness and outdoor play.


Tech Stack

Unity, C#, Vuforia


Check out the devpost!

Results

At the end of the competition my team and I were able to successfully create a working demo of our game. While making our demo, I was able to learn how to create UI with Unity and how to connect it with backend using C#. Our hard work paid off as we won the Google MVP Community award.
